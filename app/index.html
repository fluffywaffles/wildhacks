FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Who can attend?

Any and all university students from across the country are welcome. If you are a high school student and want to attend, please send an email to team@wildhacks.org.

How much does the event cost?

Zilch. There is no application, registration, or event fee. We will provide meals, drinks, and snacks for the entirety of the event.

What's the format of the hackathon?

The hackathon will last 24 hours during which teams and individuals will work to create web, desktop, and mobile computer projects.

How big should teams be? Can I do it alone?

Teams can be up to four people. You can certainly go it alone, but if you are seeking a team, we'll provide resources prior to the event to help you find a team with a project you're interested in working on.

What should I bring?

Bring your laptop and laptop charger, a valid student ID (the name on it should match the name with which you register for the event), and, of course, your vast intellect. Do not bring alcohol, other drugs, or anything that would get you in trouble if you were to pass through airport security with it (knives, etc.).

How do I apply?

Simply click on the application link and complete the application. Make sure to apply with your .edu email address.

Are we allowed to build on past projects? Can I start working on my idea before the event?

Building on past projects is strongly discouraged; in fact, we ask that you inform us if you will be using a significant amount of old code. Third-party APIs and libraries are fair game, though, so long as they are publicly and widely available.

How does the judging work?

The categories for judging and prizes will be: creativity, polish, and usefulness. There will also likely be a category for the 'people's choice'. There will be a preliminary round of judging in which all projects will be presented simultaneously in the spirit of a science-fair. Judges will circulate during this time and select groups to push through to the final round of judging, which will take place in a stage in front of a large audience.

Will WildHacks be reimbursing travel to the hackathon?

Unfortunately, it does not look like we are going to be able to provide travel reimbursement. We may be sending busses to select universities nearby - please contact us at team@wildhacks.org if you think you would be a good school to send a bus to.

I have a question that isn't listed here. Whom should I ask?

Please email any questions to team@wildhacks.org
